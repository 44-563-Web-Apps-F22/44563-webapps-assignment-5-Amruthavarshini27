Dragonflies

Dragonflies are predatory insects, both in their aquatic nymphs stage (also known as naiads) and as adults. Dragonflies are represented in human culture on artefacts such as pottery, rock paintings, statues and Art Nouveau jewellery.

Owlflies

Owlflies are readily distinguished from the superficially similar dragonflies by their long, clubbed antennae; dragonflies have short, bristle-like antennae.

Roaches(or cockroaches) are a paraphyletic group of insects belonging to Blattodea, containing all members of the group except termites. About 30 cockroach species out of 4,600 are associated with human habitats. Some species are well-known as pests.
